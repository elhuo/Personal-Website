About Me

Hiii! My name is Ellie Huo and welcome to my website! I am an incoming sophomore at the University of California San Diego. I am majoring in computer science here's a bit of how I got into CS.


I don't have a story where I found out I just really loved coding or my parents found out I was really talented with technology when I was 6 years old. But it was in junior/senior year of high school when everyone already had a major in mind but I was still debating between law or bio. In this huge decision which everyone kept telling me was soo important, I reflected on what I was passionate about. To be honest, I never was a passionate kid about anything in particular. Even my best pal "Art" which is the only thing I was willing to go to outside classes for (sorry piano, taekwando, guitar...) was not something I imagined myself doing as a career because I didn't want something I enjoyed doing that I used to relieve stress to bring me stress. And reflecting on these things, I knew I liked math or at least STEM subjects and I took AP CS in my last year of high school.


It opened up my eyes and answered a ton of questions I've always had about technology. As a kid, I've always been curious about everything and especially tech. How does wifi work? Why is it when I click a button it redirects me to another site? And also questions like the purpose of life (but thankfully God met me). Now, my questions are not all answered just because of one class but it started turning some gears in my head. The fact that everything is related to technology somehow makes my brain factory start producing a lot more questions and also many more perspectives. When the street lights outside my window turn on at night, I wonder how it was programmed to turn on at a certain time before dark and then turn off. When I play video games and I kill an enemy, I wonder behind my screen, all the calculations of damage that are being made. So I chose CS as my college major and I definitely struggle A LOT. But as it brings me new challenges, I can seek for new answers.
